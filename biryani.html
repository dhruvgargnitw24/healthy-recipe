Chicken Biryani Recipe

Calories

1 serving (1 cup): 400-500 calories depending on ingredients and preparation style.

Health Benifits

  1. Rich in Protein: Chicken provides a great source of lean protein, essential for muscle building and repair.
  2. High in Vitamins and Minerals: Chicken contains important nutrients like B-vitamins, iron, and zinc, which support overall health.
  3. Good for Immunity: The spices used in biryani, such as turmeric, garlic, and ginger, have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, helping boost immunity.
  4. Provides Energy: The rice in biryani is a good source of carbohydrates, providing energy for daily activities.
  5. Supports Heart Health: Biryani contains healthy fats (from oil or ghee), which help maintain good cholesterol levels when used in moderation.
  6. Good for Bone Health: The calcium and phosphorus from chicken, along with the aromatic spices, contribute to healthy bones.
  7. Helps in Digestion: The use of digestive spices like cumin, cloves, and cinnamon aids in better digestion.

Instructions to Make

  1. Ingredients:
    • 500g chicken, cut into pieces
    • 2 cups basmati rice
    • 1 large onion, sliced
    • 2 tomatoes, chopped
    • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
    • 1/2 cup yogurt
    • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 1 teaspoon garam masala
    • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
    • 4-5 cloves, 2-3 cardamom pods, and 1-inch cinnamon stick (whole spices)
    • 2 bay leaves
    • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
    • Salt to taste
    • Fresh coriander and mint leaves for garnish
    • Water (for cooking rice and chicken)
  2. Instructions:
    1. Marinate the Chicken: In a bowl, combine chicken pieces, yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes.
    2. Cook Rice: Rinse the basmati rice and cook it with whole spices (bay leaves, cloves, cardamom, cinnamon) and water until 70% cooked. Drain the water and set the rice aside.
    3. Cook Chicken: In a pan, heat ghee or oil. Add cumin seeds and saut√© the whole spices. Add sliced onions and cook until golden brown. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft.
    4. Combine Chicken and Rice: Add the marinated chicken to the pan with onions and tomatoes. Cook the chicken until it is 70-80% cooked. Then layer the cooked rice over the chicken.
    5. Simmer: Cover the pan with a tight lid and cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes (dum cooking). Optionally, add some saffron-soaked milk on top of the rice for flavor.
    6. Serve: Garnish the biryani with fresh coriander and mint leaves. Serve hot with raita or salad.
