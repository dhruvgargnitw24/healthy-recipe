Dosa Recipe

Calories

A basic plain dosa contains around 120-150 calories, but this can increase with additional ingredients like oil, ghee, or fillings.

Health Benifits

  1. Rich in Carbohydrates: Provides long-lasting energy.
  2. Good Source of Protein: Offers plant-based protein from urad dal.
  3. Improves Digestion: Contains probiotics from fermentation.
  4. Low in Fat: Healthy, low-fat option when cooked with minimal oil.
  5. Gluten-Free: Ideal for those with gluten intolerance or celiac disease.
  6. Rich in Fiber: Supports healthy digestion and heart health.
  7. Low Glycemic Index: Helps maintain stable blood sugar levels.
  8. Vitamins and Minerals: Provides iron, calcium, magnesium, and B-vitamins.
  9. Helps in Weight Management: Keeps you full for longer due to high fiber content.

Instructions to Make

  1. Ingredients:
    • 1 cup rice
    • 1/4 cup urad dal
    • 1/4 tsp fenugreek seeds (optional)
    • Water
    • Salt to taste
  2. Instructions:
    1. Soak: Soak rice and urad dal (with fenugreek) for 6-8 hours or overnight.
    2. Grind: Grind the rice and urad dal separately into smooth and slightly coarse batters using water. Mix both batters and add salt.
    3. Ferment: Let the batter ferment for 8-12 hours at room temperature.
    4. Cook: Heat a greased pan. Pour the batter and spread it thinly. Cook until golden brown and crispy.
    5. Serve: Serve with chutney or sambar.
