Rajma Rice Recipe

Calories

A typical serving of Rajma Rice contains about 425-515 calories per serving, depending on how much oil or ghee is used for cooking. The dish is filling, nutritious, and provides a good mix of carbohydrates, protein, and fiber.

Health Benifits

  1. Rich in Protein: Rajma (kidney beans) is a great plant-based protein source, essential for muscle growth and repair.
  2. High in Fiber: Rajma and rice together provide dietary fiber, aiding in digestion and promoting a healthy gut.
  3. Good for Heart Health: Rajma contains antioxidants and heart-healthy compounds that help manage cholesterol levels.
  4. Helps in Weight Management: The high fiber and protein content help keep you full for longer, reducing overeating.
  5. Rich in Iron: Rajma is a good source of iron, which supports healthy blood circulation and prevents anemia.
  6. Regulates Blood Sugar: The low glycemic index of rajma helps stabilize blood sugar levels, making it diabetic-friendly.
  7. Loaded with Vitamins & Minerals: Rajma is rich in B-vitamins, calcium, and magnesium, supporting overall health.

Instructions to Make

  1. Ingredients:
    • 1 cup rajma (kidney beans)
    • 1 cup basmati rice
    • 1 onion, chopped
    • 1 tomato, chopped
    • 2-3 garlic cloves, minced
    • 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
    • 1 green chili (optional), chopped
    • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
    • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 1 teaspoon garam masala
    • Salt to taste
    • Oil or ghee for cooking
    • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  2. Instructions:
    1. Soak the Rajma: Soak rajma in water for at least 6-8 hours or overnight.
    2. Cook Rajma: Drain and pressure cook the soaked rajma with water and a pinch of salt for 3-4 whistles, until soft.
    3. Prepare Rice: Rinse the rice thoroughly and cook it in a pot or rice cooker with 2 cups of water until soft and fluffy.
    4. Prepare the Rajma Gravy: In a pan, heat oil/ghee. Add cumin seeds, followed by chopped onions, ginger, garlic, and green chili. Saut√© until golden brown.
    5. Cook Spices: Add turmeric powder, garam masala, and chopped tomatoes. Cook until the tomatoes soften.
    6. Combine Rajma and Gravy: Add the cooked rajma to the pan with the spices. Mix well, add salt, and simmer for 5-10 minutes.
    7. Serve: Serve the rajma gravy over the cooked rice, garnished with fresh coriander leaves.
