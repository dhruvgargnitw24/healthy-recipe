Dal Roti Recipe

Calories

A typical serving of Dal Roti contains around 350-400 calories, depending on the amount of oil or ghee used.

Health Benefits

  1. High in Protein: Dal provides plant-based protein, essential for muscle growth and repair.
  2. Rich in Fiber: Whole wheat roti and dal provide fiber for digestive health.
  3. Low in Fat: When prepared with minimal oil, dal and roti make for a healthy, low-fat meal.

Instructions to Make

  1. Ingredients:
    • 1 cup yellow dal (lentils)
    • 1 onion, chopped
    • 1 tomato, chopped
    • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
    • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
    • 1 tablespoon ghee or oil
    • Salt to taste
    • Whole wheat flour (for roti)
  2. Instructions:
    1. Cook Dal: Wash and cook dal with turmeric powder and salt until soft.
    2. Prepare Tadka (Tempering): Heat ghee, add cumin seeds, onions, and cook until golden. Add tomatoes, red chili powder, and cook until soft.
    3. Combine Dal and Tadka: Add the tempering to the cooked dal, stir, and cook for a few more minutes.
    4. Prepare Roti: Mix whole wheat flour with water and a pinch of salt, knead into a dough, and roll out into flat rotis.
    5. Cook Roti: Cook the rotis on a hot tawa until golden brown on both sides.
    6. Serve: Serve the dal with roti and a side of salad or pickle.
